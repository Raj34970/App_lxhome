üîç What is a Network Switch?

A network switch is a device that connects multiple computers, servers, printers, or other hardware on a Local Area Network (LAN). It intelligently forwards data to the correct destination by reading hardware (MAC) addresses, unlike hubs that broadcast data to all devices. Put simply: a switch is the traffic controller of your network, ensuring your data gets where it needs to go‚Äîfast and efficiently.

  • Speed:Switches support high-speed data transfer, typically 1Gbps, 10Gbps, or even faster.

  • Reliability:Keeps network traffic organized and prevents collisions.
  • Scalability:Easily expand your network as your needs grow.
  • Security:Managed switches offer VLANs, port control, and traffic monitoring for better protection.
